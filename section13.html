第十三章 · 劝持品

妙法莲华经

← 第十二章 目录 Contents 第十四章 →

尔时，药王菩萨摩诃萨及大乐说菩萨摩诃萨，与二万菩萨眷属俱，皆于佛前，而作是言：唯然，世尊，我等亦当于如来灭后，十方国土，广说此经。所以者何？是阎浮提人，刚强难化，多增上慢，贪于功德，增益我见，佛灭度后，若有比丘宣说此经，必多怨嫉，加以毁谤，刀杖瓦石，常被击打，佐助求瑕，白衣比丘，而见轻慢。有诸比丘尼，诸优婆塞，诸优婆夷，种种毁骂。我等皆当忍是，广说此经。

逐句解释

尔时，药王菩萨摩诃萨及大乐说菩萨摩诃萨，与二万菩萨眷属俱，皆于佛前，而作是言：唯然，世尊，我等亦当于如来灭后，十方国土，广说此经。

这一幕是《法华经》中最具动力的场景之一。药王菩萨（药王，医药之王，象征拯救与疗愈）和大乐说菩萨（大乐，极乐，象征最高的喜乐与解脱）率领二万菩萨，一起在佛陀面前发言。他们说：「唯然，世尊。」——「是的，世尊。」——用最简洁有力的方式表达了他们的理解和承诺。然后他们声明：我们也要在如来涅槃之后，在十方国土，广泛地传讲这部经。这不是命令，而是自发的誓愿。他们看到了什么，以至于这样燃烧地站出来？

Bhaisajya Bodhisattva the Great and Great Visvabharanirghosa Bodhisattva, together with two myriad other bodhisattvas, stand before the Buddha and say: Yes, World-Honoured One! We too shall, after the Tathagata's passing, throughout the ten-directional lands, broadly expound this sutra.

The bodhisattvas are not waiting to be asked. Their rising is spontaneous, driven by their own clarity about the teaching's value and their commitment to the welfare of beings. The words "yes, world-honoured one" (唯然，世尊) are deceptively simple — they are complete affirmation. What these bodhisattvas have understood, by this point in the sutra, is that the one vehicle is real; that every being will reach Buddhahood; and that this truth is so precious that it must be guarded and transmitted at any cost. Their spontaneity is the sign that the teaching has truly landed — not imposed from outside, but awakening action from within.

所以者何？是阎浮提人，刚强难化，多增上慢，贪于功德，增益我见，佛灭度后，若有比丘宣说此经，必多怨嫉，加以毁谤，刀杖瓦石，常被击打，佐助求瑕，白衣比丘，而见轻慢。

菩萨们解释为什么要这样做：原因很明确。「阎浮提」指的是我们所在的世界，其特点是「刚强难化」——人们性格倔强，很难被教化。他们充满「增上慢」——自以为是的骄傲，自认为已经明白。他们贪着功德的形式，执著于「我见」——对自我的固执。在这样的世界里，一旦有人开始讲《法华经》，立刻会招来怨恨、嫉妒、诽谤。不仅如此，传讲这部经的人会被物理上的暴力所伤害——遭到刀剑、棍棒、瓦片、石头的攻击。甚至连其他修行者也会来找他们的麻烦，佛教的出家人和在家信徒都会轻视他们。

Why is this so? The people of Jambudvipa are stubborn and difficult to teach. They are full of pride and arrogance, greedy for merit, attached to the view of self. After the Buddha's passing, if monks proclaim this sutra, they will inevitably face resentment and jealousy, slander and contempt, and physical violence — struck by swords, staves, tiles, and stones. Both ordained and lay practitioners will seek their faults and despise them.

The bodhisattvas are brutally honest about what awaits them. Jambudvipa — the world of human beings, with all its stubbornness and self-deception — is not a hospitable place for the truth. The word 刚強 (stubborn, rigid) suggests a kind of hardness that resists the soft touch of wisdom. People are full of 增上慢 (arrogant conceit) — they think they already know. So when someone comes with a teaching that suggests they are wrong, that even their enemies can become Buddhas, that the path is one and not many — they react with hostility. The Lotus Sutra acknowledges this unflinchingly. It does not promise safety for its messengers. It promises meaning.

有诸比丘尼，诸优婆塞，诸优婆夷，种种毁骂。我等皆当忍是，广说此经。

尽管面临着这样的困境，菩萨们的回应只有七个字：「我等皆当忍是，广说此经。」——我们都要忍耐这一切，并继续广泛地宣讲这部经。这是菩萨道最精粹的表达：看得清楚、说得明白，然后选择前行。不是逃避困难，而是知难而进。不是自欺欺人地说「没有困难」，而是说「困难来了，我们接受，我们继续」。

Both nuns and male and female laypeople will revile them with various abuses. All of us will endure this and will broadly teach this sutra.

The phrase 忍是 (endure this) carries the full weight of the bodhisattva path. "Endure" is not passive resignation — it is active commitment in the face of difficulty. The bodhisattva path, as depicted here, is not an escape into spiritual comfort. It is a choice to stay present with the world's pain and confusion, to offer what one understands, and to accept whatever reaction comes. It is a choice made with open eyes, without self-pity or resentment.

总结 · Summary

第十三章是一章誓愿之书。数万菩萨、六千比丘尼，在知道将面临诽谤、暴力和排斥的情况下，主动立誓：佛灭度后，我们要在各地广传《法华经》。这种知难而进的精神，是大乘菩萨行的核心。「忍」不是软弱，而是有力量的慈悲。《法华经》通过这一章告诉我们：真正的传法，从来都不是容易的事，但它永远值得。

Chapter 13 is a chapter of vows. Tens of thousands of bodhisattvas and six thousand nuns — including Mahaprajapati and Yashodhara — step forward voluntarily and commit to teaching the Lotus Sutra after the Buddha's death, in full knowledge of the hostility they will face. Slander, physical violence, social exclusion — they name each difficulty and vow to endure it anyway. This is the bodhisattva path made concrete and costly: not an ideal held at safe distance, but a commitment made in clear light. The chapter stands as a tribute to every person in every age who has transmitted a difficult, unwelcome truth for the benefit of beings who were not yet ready to receive it.
