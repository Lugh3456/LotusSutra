第十四章 · 安乐行品

妙法莲华经

← 第十三章 目录 Contents 第十五章 →

文殊师利，菩萨摩诃萨，于后末世，法欲灭时，受持读诵斯经典者，无怀嫉妒谄诳之心，亦勿轻骂学佛道者，求其长短。若比丘比丘尼，优婆塞优婆夷，求声闻者，求辟支佛者，求菩萨道者，无得恼之，令生疑惑。菩萨住是第一安乐行，能于后世读诵是经，多有安乐。又文殊师利，如来灭后，末法之中，欲说此经，应住安乐行。若口所演，若心所念，无有粗犷，心常柔软，亦勿轻蔑诃骂有所缺漏，深达实相，亦不轻动小乘学者之心。

逐句解释

文殊师利，菩萨摩诃萨，于后末世，法欲灭时，受持读诵斯经典者，无怀嫉妒谄诳之心，亦勿轻骂学佛道者，求其长短。

文殊菩萨（文殊师利）代表了智慧。佛陀开始向他讲解，针对的是「后末世，法欲灭时」——一个遥远的未来，法即将灭没的时代。在这样的时代里，要受持和传讲《法华经》，需要什么样的品质？首先，要「无怀嫉妒谄诳之心」——不要怀有嫉妒和欺诈的心。嫉妒的心会让你看不到他人的优点，谄诳的心会让你失去真诚。其次，「亦勿轻骂学佛道者，求其长短」——不要轻视或辱骂那些学习佛道的人，也不要去寻求他们的过失和不足。

Manjushri, supreme bodhisattva! In the final age, when the dharma is about to perish, those who uphold, read, and recite this sutra must not harbour envy or fraud in their hearts. Nor should they disparage those who study the Buddha way or seek out their faults and shortcomings.

The instruction begins with a prohibition: do not let these negative states take root in your being. Envy (嫉妒) and deception (謄誑) are the poisons that corrupt spiritual practice from within. To harbour them is to poison the very teaching you are trying to give. Manjushri is addressed because wisdom must be the foundation — without wisdom, you cannot see that disparaging another practitioner is disparaging yourself, because all beings are ultimately undivided in Buddha-nature. The phrase "seek out their faults" (求其長短) suggests active hunting for problems rather than passive observation. The teacher is instructed: let others' imperfections be; you have enough to do with your own.

四种安乐行——身、口、意、誓愿

「安乐行」是本章的核心概念，指传法者在末法时代应当修习的四种安稳、平静的行为准则：身安乐行（行为举止）、口安乐行（言语表达）、意安乐行（内心态度）、誓愿安乐行（发心动机）。四者合一，才是真正「安住」于传法之道。

The Four Practices of Peaceful Conduct: conduct of body, conduct of speech, conduct of mind, and conduct of vow. Together, these four constitute the bodhisattva's way of living and teaching in the difficult age after the Buddha's passing.

Chapter 14 is the Lotus Sutra's most practically oriented chapter. Where Chapter 13 described the difficulties of teaching with unflinching honesty, Chapter 14 offers a response: not retreat, but a particular quality of engagement. The four peaceful practices are essentially a complete code of conduct for a dharma teacher in adversarial conditions. They cover the external (what you do and say), the internal (what you think and feel), and the motivational (why you do it at all). Unlike Chapter 13's emphasis on endurance, Chapter 14 emphasises cultivation — the inner work that makes sustainable teaching possible.

若比丘比丘尼，优婆塞优婆夷，求声闻者，求辟支佛者，求菩萨道者，无得恼之，令生疑惑。

佛陀进一步细化了这个教导：不要去激怒或扰乱任何学习佛道的人，无论他们追求的是哪一种解脱之道——声闻乘（释迦弟子直接听闻教法）、辟支佛乘（缘觉乘，靠自己观察因缘而悟道）、或菩萨乘（追求圆满的、利益一切众生的觉悟）。「无得恼之，令生疑惑」——不要惹恼他们，不要让他们对法生起疑心。这很关键：在末法时代，人的信心容易动摇。作为传法者，你的言行举止都要尽量不动摇别人的信心，而是坚定和增长信心。

Whether monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen who seek the arhat path, the pratyekabuddha path, or the bodhisattva path — do not vex them or cause them to doubt.

This instruction acknowledges the multiplicity of Buddhist paths — not everyone will immediately embrace the One Vehicle teaching. Some will still be walking the arhat path; some the pratyekabuddha path; some the bodhisattva path. Rather than condemn these as inferior (as an exclusive teaching might), the instruction is: honour where they are. Do not shake their faith. The role of the teacher is not to demolish what others believe but to water and nourish the seeds of awakening that are already present. In the final age, people are especially vulnerable; their faith is fragile like a candle flame in wind. Your job is to shield that flame, not blow it out.

菩萨住是第一安乐行，能于后世读诵是经，多有安乐。

有了这样的内在准备之后，菩萨就「住是第一安乐行」——安住在身的安乐行中。「能于后世读诵是经，多有安乐」——如果有人在这样的清净与柔和中读诵这部经，他们会得到许多的安乐。这个安乐是什么？不是外在的享受，而是一种内在的稳定与平静——因为你不再与世界对抗，而是与世界和谐共存；不再追逐虚幻的利益，而是安住于真实的价值。

The bodhisattva is established in this first practice of peaceful conduct. One who reads and recites this sutra in this way will have abundant peace and happiness.

The word 安樂 (peace and happiness) appears repeatedly throughout this chapter. But what kind of happiness is this? It is the happiness that comes from alignment — when your actions, words, thoughts, and intentions are all pointing in the same direction, toward the benefit of beings. When you are not at war with yourself, you are not exhausted. When you are not struggling against others, you are not tense. This happiness is not the absence of difficulty; it is the presence of clarity and integrity in the midst of difficulty.

又文殊师利，如来灭后，末法之中，欲说此经，应住安乐行。

佛陀再次强调，在如来灭度之后，在末法时代，要宣讲这部经，必须依止这些安乐行。「应住安乐行」意思是「必须安住在这些行为准则中」。不是可选的，而是必需的。因为末法时代，众生的心更难调伏，环境更加险恶，如果没有这样的坚实基础，传法者很容易被冲动、愤怒、骄傲所淹没。

Furthermore, Manjushri, after the Tathagata's passing, in the age of declining dharma, if one wishes to teach this sutra, one should dwell in these practices of peaceful conduct.

The repetition and emphasis on "should dwell" (應住) transforms these guidelines from suggestions into requirements. In the final age, when conditions are more difficult and human capacity is weaker, these practices are not luxuries but necessities. They are the scaffolding that keeps the teaching standing upright when the world is shaking.

若口所演，若心所念，无有粗犷，心常柔软

口安乐行与意安乐行的核心：无论说话还是思维，都保持柔和，不粗鲁，不激烈。「柔软」不是软弱，而是一种不带攻击性的力量——像水一样，可以穿石，但不带怒气。同时，「深达实相」——基础是对诸法空性的真正理解。从空性出发，粗犷自然消融。

Whether in what you say or what you think, do not be rough or harsh. Keep your mind always gentle and soft. Deeply penetrate the nature of reality — and do not disturb or unsettle the minds of those who follow the lesser path.

The conduct of speech and mind are closely linked here. The word 柔軟 (gentle, soft) appears repeatedly in this chapter — it is the keynote quality of the peaceful practitioner. This does not mean avoiding difficult truths; it means communicating them without the friction of ego, anger, or contempt. The instruction to "deeply penetrate the nature of reality" (深達實相) grounds the other practices: gentleness that arises from genuine insight into emptiness is stable, because it doesn't depend on the other person cooperating. It is not conditional on being received well. It is simply the natural expression of seeing clearly.

髻中明珠喻——转轮圣王的最高赏赐

章末，佛陀用「髻珠喻」（第七个大比喻）来描述《法华经》的珍贵程度：一位转轮圣王在战胜诸国之后，把各种宝物赏赐给将士，唯独发髻中的那颗明珠，他始终没有给——因为太珍贵了。直到最后，他才把这颗珠子赏给功劳最大的人。《法华经》就是那颗髻中明珠。

The Buddha compares the Lotus Sutra to the brilliant jewel hidden in a wheel-turning king's topknot. The king gave away all his other treasures freely to his victorious warriors — but this jewel, the most precious of all, he kept until last, giving it only to the most meritorious. The Lotus Sutra is that jewel.

The Parable of the King's Topknot Jewel (髻珠喻) is the seventh and final major parable of the Lotus Sutra. A cakravartin (wheel-turning king, 轉輪聖王) is a mythological universal monarch of immense power and virtue. The fact that he withheld his most precious jewel throughout all his other distributions of reward mirrors the Buddha's withholding of the Lotus Sutra's central teaching (the One Vehicle) until late in his teaching career. Everything else — the other sutras, the other teachings — were given freely and early. But this teaching, this jewel, waited for the right moment, the right audience, the right level of preparation. And now it is given.

总结 · Summary

第十四章从誓愿的激情回到了日常修行的细节。佛陀向文殊菩萨解说四种「安乐行」：以柔和、无嫉妒、不轻慢他人的方式行事、说话、思想和发愿。这不是软弱，而是有深厚根基的平静——来自对空性的真实理解。章末以「髻珠喻」收尾：《法华经》是转轮圣王发髻中最珍贵的宝珠，是所有教法中最后、也是最深的赐予。

Chapter 14 is the Lotus Sutra's guide to the inner life of a dharma teacher. The four peaceful practices — of body, speech, mind, and vow — describe a way of living in the world that is both engaged and undefended, active and gentle, committed and spacious. The foundation is not willpower or discipline but genuine insight: when you see clearly into emptiness, roughness falls away naturally. The chapter closes with the seventh great parable — the king's topknot jewel — placing the Lotus Sutra itself as the most precious and most carefully timed of all the Buddha's gifts. He gave everything else first; this, he saved for last.
