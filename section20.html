第二十章 · 常不轻菩萨品

妙法莲华经

← 第十九章 目录 Contents 第二十一章 →

得大势，乃往古昔，过无量无边不可思议阿僧祇劫，有佛名威音王如来。其佛灭后，像法之中，有一增上慢比丘，势力甚大。尔时有一菩萨比丘，名常不轻。得大势，以何因缘，名常不轻？是比丘，凡有所见，若比丘比丘尼，优婆塞优婆夷，皆悉礼拜赞叹，而作是言：我深敬汝等，不敢轻慢，所以者何？汝等皆行菩萨道，当得作佛。而是比丘，不专读诵经典，但行礼拜，乃至远见四众，亦复故往礼拜赞叹，而作是言：我不敢轻于汝等，汝等皆当作佛。

逐句解释

得大势，乃往古昔，过无量无边不可思议阿僧祇劫，有佛名威音王如来

第二十章讲述了一个古老的故事。在无量不可思议的劫数之前，有一位叫「威音王」的佛陀。整个故事——关于常不轻菩萨的——发生在那个远古时代。这样的叙述方式表明：我们现在讲述的不是简单的历史故事，而是贯穿整个宇宙历史的、具有永恒意义的一个范型。

Bodhisattva Mahasattva Dashizhi, in ancient times long past, through immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable asamkhya-kalpas, there was a Buddha named Tathagata of Sovereign Resonance (Vipaśyin Buddha).

The temporal vastness — "immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable asamkhya-kalpas" — establishes immediately that this story exists outside historical time. It is a mythological or archetypal narrative, not a historical chronicle. The Buddha's name, "威音王" (Vipaśyin in Sanskrit), means one whose voice or sound is sovereign — who speaks with authority. The story that follows is about what happens in such a Buddha's world, and it will provide a template for understanding the eternal structure of how truth is transmitted.

其佛灭后，像法之中，有一增上慢比丘，势力甚大

威音王佛陀涅槃之后，佛法进入了「像法」时代——真法衰落、似法兴盛的时代。在这样的时代里，出现了一位「增上慢比丘」，他虽然持戒严谨，但傲慢心很重，自以为高明，拒绝向他人虚心学习。他的势力很大，有很多弟子跟随。这个人物象征着对法的错误执取。

After that Buddha had entered parinirvana, during the age of the Dharma's semblance, there was a monk of high conceit who had great power and influence. He observed the precepts, but his heart harboured arrogance and pride; he refused to accept the teachings of elders.

"增上慢" — high conceit, or pride of superior understanding — is a particular form of spiritual delusion. It is not ordinary arrogance (which a practitioner might admit to and work with), but a convinced sense of one's own understanding. The monk thinks he has grasped the dharma correctly and others have gone astray. This makes him rigid, dismissive of other perspectives, unable to learn. The fact that he has many followers amplifies the problem: his rigidity becomes institutionalized. This is the Lotus Sutra's diagnosis of one particular way the Dharma becomes distorted: not through outright rejection, but through confident misunderstanding.

尔时有一菩萨比丘，名常不轻

在那个时代，出现了一位叫「常不轻」的菩萨比丘——他的名字本身就揭示了他的本质：总是不轻视他人。他与傲慢的增上慢比丘形成了鲜明的对比。常不轻的存在本身就是一个反抗：反抗对他人的贬低，反抗骄慢心，反抗对法的僵化理解。

At that time there was a bodhisattva-monk named Never Disparaging. This bodhisattva-monk, whenever he met any of the four groups of disciples — monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen — would bow respectfully and praise them, saying...

The name "常不轻" — Never Disparaging, or Constantly Respectful — is not an external title but a description of his essential practice. Every action embodies respect. He does not wait to assess whether people are worthy of respect; respect is his fundamental stance toward all beings. This is the inverse of the arrogant monk: where arrogance sees deficiency and judges, respect sees potential and honours. These two figures — the arrogant monk with great power and the humble monk with a simple practice — are the two poles of the Dharma's distortion and preservation.

得大势，以何因缘，名常不轻？是比丘，凡有所见，若比丘比丘尼，优婆塞优婆夷，皆悉礼拜赞叹，而作是言：我深敬汝等，不敢轻慢，所以者何？汝等皆行菩萨道，当得作佛

佛陀向得大势菩萨解释：常不轻之所以得名，是因为他看到任何人——比丘、比丘尼、居士、女居士——都会合掌礼拜、赞叹他们，说出那个著名的句子：「我深敬汝等，不敢轻慢。汝等皆行菩萨道，当得作佛。」他的礼拜不是客套，而是源于对每一个众生内在佛性的深信。

Dasizhi, why was this monk named Never Disparaging? This bodhisattva-monk, whenever he encountered any of the four groups of disciples, would bow respectfully to all of them and praise them, saying: "I deeply respect you all, I would never dare hold you in contempt. Why? Because you are all practising the bodhisattva path and will all attain Buddhahood."

The key teaching: Never Disparaging's respect is not based on external assessment of people's current attainment. He does not wait to see if they are ethical, wise, or accomplished. He bows to everyone, because his vision penetrates to a truth deeper than current appearance: the Buddha-nature that all beings possess. This is not naïve optimism; it is metaphysical sight. He sees what is true — that all beings have the capacity for Buddhahood — and his behaviour (bowing, speaking respectfully) flows naturally from that seeing. In doing so, he is not flattering; he is recognising.

汝等皆行菩萨道，当得作佛

这个句子值得特别关注，因为它是常不轻菩萨反复述说的核心内容。每一次遇见众生，他都会重申这个信念：你们都在行菩萨道，你们都会成佛。这个重复的、一成不变的宣言不是重复无聊的说教，而是一种禅定式的坚持——用不断的重申来改变他人的自我认知。

You are all practising the bodhisattva path and will all attain Buddhahood.

This single sentence, repeated without variation to every person without exception, is the entire content of Never Disparaging's teaching. He does not cite sutras, does not elaborate philosophy, does not tailor his message. The same five words, again and again. This constancy itself is the teaching. In a world of relativism and doubt, in an age when rigorous monks are insisting that some beings lack Buddha-nature (a historical heresy), Never Disparaging's unshakeable proclamation creates a counter-current. His words are a mirror held up to beings: not "I think you could become a Buddha," but "You are already a Buddha-becoming," affirmed as stable reality.

而是比丘，不专读诵经典，但行礼拜，乃至远见四众，亦复故往礼拜赞叹，而作是言：我不敢轻于汝等，汝等皆当作佛

常不轻菩萨的修行方式令人印象深刻：他不专门读诵经典——这在当时比丘的日常修行中是罕见的缺陷——但他坚持礼拜。即使看到远处有人，他也会走过去，礼拜、赞叹，说出那个永恒的句子。他的修行纲领不是知识，而是行动；不是理解，而是尊敬；不是修养，而是坚持。

This bodhisattva-monk did not specialise in the study and recitation of Buddhist scriptures, but instead devoted himself to bowing in respect. Even when he saw the four groups from afar, he would deliberately go to them, bow down, praise them, and speak his words: "I dare not disparage you; you will all become Buddhas."

The detail that he did not specialise in scripture study is paradoxical in a monastic context — and intentionally so. The Lotus Sutra is here pointing at a truth that transcends doctrinal knowledge: the most profound practice is not about accumulating information about enlightenment but about embodying one truth through action. Never Disparaging's life is a koan: how can someone who doesn't study the sutras be a bodhisattva? The answer: because his entire being has become a transmission. Every bow is a sermon; every word is a Buddha-proclamation. The sutra is not something he knows; it has become something he is.

常不轻菩萨——「我不敢轻于汝等，汝等皆当作佛」

「常不轻菩萨」是《法华经》最感人的人物之一。他的名字来自他的行为：他见到任何人——比丘、比丘尼、居士、女居士——都会合掌礼拜，说：「我不敢轻视你们，因为你们都在行菩萨道，都将成佛。」他的整个修行，就是这一句话和这一个礼拜。

Never Disparaging Bodhisattva (常不轻) received his name from his practice: whenever he met any person — monk, nun, layman, or laywoman — he would bow to them and say: "I deeply respect you. I would not dare hold you in contempt. Why? Because you are all practising the bodhisattva path and will attain Buddhahood."

The practice of Never Disparaging Bodhisattva is the Lotus Sutra's most radical statement about how to treat other beings. His entire spiritual practice consists of a single gesture and a single sentence, repeated without variation to every person he meets. He does not select the worthy; he bows to everyone. He does not require evidence of their practice; he sees the seed of Buddhahood in all of them, and acts accordingly. This is the practical application of the sutra's central teaching: if every being will become a Buddha, then every being deserves the respect due to a future Buddha — right now, exactly as they are.

他被毁骂、投石——仍然礼拜，从远处说

人们不接受他的礼拜，有的嘲笑他，有的辱骂他，有人甚至用杖棒和石头打他。但常不轻菩萨从不反击、从不怨恨——他只是走远一点，继续从远处合掌说：「我不敢轻于汝等，汝等皆当作佛。」攻击他的人骂他「狂人」，他仍然礼拜。这种坚持不是固执，而是不可动摇的慈悲。

People mocked him, insulted him, struck him with staffs and stones. Never Disparaging did not retaliate. He would simply move to a distance and continue calling out his salutation: "I would not dare hold you in contempt — you will all become Buddhas!" Those who struck him called him crazy. He still bowed to them.

The violence directed at Never Disparaging is not incidental; it is the test. His practice is only meaningful because it is maintained under assault. Anyone can respect people who respect them back; it takes a different order of realisation to continue bowing to someone who is throwing stones at you. The fact that he moves to a distance — to avoid being struck — while continuing his salutation is a detail of quiet wisdom: he is not a masochist seeking punishment, nor is he a pushover who has no self-preservation. He is someone who has simply decided that the truth about every being's Buddha-nature is more important than any particular person's current behaviour toward him.

临命终时，得闻《法华经》，寿命延长，广为说法

常不轻菩萨在临终时，忽然从虚空中听到了威音王佛所说的《法华经》，当下全部受持。他的寿命因此延长，继续为众生广说《法华经》，那些曾经迫害他的人，最终也因此获益，踏上了成佛之路。这个转折充满了慈悲的讽刺：迫害者，最终成为受益者。

As Never Disparaging approached death, he heard the voice of Many Treasures Buddha reciting the Lotus Sutra from empty space, received it completely, and his lifespan was extended. He then spent many more years teaching the sutra — and those who had beaten and mocked him eventually received the teaching from him and attained Buddhahood.

The denouement is theologically perfect. The people who attacked him are not punished; they are educated. Their violence against him did not disqualify them from the Dharma — nothing does. And the teacher who brings them to the Dharma is the very person they abused. Never Disparaging's years of bowing to them, of insisting on their future Buddhahood even as they threw stones, was itself a form of planting seeds. The seeds germinated. When the sutra was eventually taught directly, those beings were ready to receive it — in part because of the long, strange preparation of being respected by someone they treated with contempt.

常不轻菩萨，即是释迦牟尼前身

佛陀最后揭示：常不轻菩萨正是他自己的前身。正是这种「礼敬一切众生」的修行，奠定了他成佛的基础。这个揭示意味深长：成佛的关键，不是苦行或智识，而是对每个生命不可动摇的尊重与信任。见到每个人的佛性——即便他们还不知道——就是菩萨行。

The Buddha reveals: Never Disparaging Bodhisattva was none other than myself in a past life. It was through that practice of bowing to every being and affirming their future Buddhahood that I laid the foundation for my own awakening.

This final revelation reframes the entire chapter as autobiography. The practice of Never Disparaging — so simple, so repetitive, so apparently naive — is identified as the decisive practice of a future Buddha. Not the mastery of abstruse philosophy, not heroic asceticism, not miraculous powers. Bowing. Saying "you will become a Buddha" to every person you meet, and meaning it, no matter how they respond. The teaching is stark in its implication: if you want to know what a Buddha does, this is it. See the seed of awakening in every face. Act accordingly. Do not stop, no matter what.

总结 · Summary

常不轻菩萨的故事是《法华经》最动人的篇章之一。他没有博学的教义，没有神奇的法力——他只有一个礼拜和一句话：「我不敢轻于汝等，汝等皆当作佛。」他在嘲笑、辱骂、石头中，从未停止。最后，他自己成了佛，那些打过他的人也因他的持续尊重而最终得度。佛陀揭示：这就是他自己的前身。成佛的根本，是不可动摇地尊重每一个众生的佛性。

The story of Never Disparaging Bodhisattva is perhaps the most beloved in the entire Lotus Sutra. His practice is disarmingly simple: bow to everyone, tell them they will become Buddhas, and keep doing it no matter how they respond. Mocked, beaten, and called crazy, he never retaliates and never gives up. His persecutors eventually become his students, and he eventually becomes — as the Buddha reveals — Shakyamuni himself. The teaching is the story: the path to Buddhahood runs through unconditional respect for the Buddha-nature in every being. Not wisdom, not power, not knowledge. Seeing clearly what is true about others, and acting on that vision with absolute consistency.
