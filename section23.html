尔时宿王华菩萨白佛言：世尊，药王菩萨云何游于娑婆世界？世尊，是药王菩萨有若干百千万亿那由他难行苦行。善哉，世尊，愿少解说。佛告宿王华菩萨：乃往古昔，过无量恒河沙劫，有佛号日月净明德如来。其佛有菩萨，名一切众生喜见。是菩萨乐习苦行，于日月净明德佛法中，精进经行，一心求佛，满万二千岁已，得现一切色身三昧。得此三昧已，心大欢喜，即作念言：我得现一切色身三昧，皆是得闻法华经力。我今当供养日月净明德佛。即以神力，雨众名华，及以香雨，绕佛三匝，以头面礼足，合掌恭敬，烧身供佛。

逐句解释

尔时宿王华菩萨白佛言：世尊，药王菩萨云何游于娑婆世界？

宿王华菩萨向佛陀提问：「药王菩萨为何来游化娑婆世界？」这个问题开启了第二十三章的序章。「云何」——怎样、为什么、以什么方式。提问者不是无知者，而是想要激发佛陀的教说。这个提问方式在《法华经》中屡见不鲜，它是一种温和的引导——聆听者通过提问来帮助全众进入理解。

Then Sudhanakumara Bodhisattva asked the Buddha: "Honored One, in what way does Medicine King Bodhisattva traverse the world of Saha (our world)?"

The question is the literary device that opens the chapter's narrative. Sudhanakumara — whose name means "Prince of Auspicious Moon" — is functioning here as a representative of the assembly and also as a stand-in for the reader. His question is not naive; it is a skillful invitation for the Buddha to unfold the story of Medicine King's attainment. This pattern of asking and answering is fundamental to the sutra's pedagogy: wisdom is not downloaded but elicited, drawn out through dialogue.

世尊，是药王菩萨有若干百千万亿那由他难行苦行

宿王华菩萨继续说：「世尊啊，药王菩萨曾经历过数不尽的百千万亿那由他次的难行苦行。」这句话既是叹息，也是追问：什么样的修行，才能成就药王菩萨这样的力量？他要求的不只是一个答案，而是一个故事——一个可以激励后学者的实例。

"Honored One, Medicine King Bodhisattva has undergone immeasurable and inconceivable austerities and difficult practices."

The phrase "难行苦行" — difficult practices and bitter austerities — is not mere admiration but a kind of awed questioning. How does one attain such spiritual power? Through what effort? The use of the word 难 (hard, difficult) in combination with 苦 (bitter, difficult) emphasizes that the path is not for the faint-hearted. Yet Sudhanakumara is inviting the assembly to look at this very difficulty, not as a deterrent, but as the signature of genuine spiritual transformation. Easy attainment would not convince us; difficulty does.

佛告宿王华菩萨：乃往古昔，过无量恒河沙劫，有佛号日月净明德如来

佛陀开始讲述药王菩萨的往昔故事。「乃往古昔」——在遥远的过去；「过无量恒河沙劫」——超越了恒河沙那样无数的劫数。时间被推到了无限远处，暗示我们即将听到的不是一个普通的历史事件，而是跨越无数时代的因缘流传。那个时代有一位佛名叫「日月净明德」，寓意光明与纯净，这个名号本身就在为这个故事设置精神背景。

The Buddha told Sudhanakumara: "In the distant past, beyond immeasurable kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, there was a Buddha named Tathagata of Pure Radiance of Sun and Moon."

The narrative convention of beginning a past-life story is itself a form of teaching. By placing the story so far in the past — beyond counting in terms of kalpas and Ganges-sand metaphors — the sutra establishes that what we are about to hear is not local to one time or place. It happened across vast time, which means it can happen again, and again, in every age. The Buddha's name — Sun and Moon Pure Radiance — is deliberately chosen: light that illuminates day and night, that measures the cycles of time, that never fails. We are being prepared to hear a story about the transformation of time and dedication itself.

其佛有菩萨，名一切众生喜见

在那位佛的教化中，有一位菩萨，名叫「一切众生喜见」（Bodhisattva All-Beings-Delighted-to-See）。这个名号本身就描述了他的精神境界：他的存在本身就会令所有众生欢喜。没有嗔恨，没有冷漠，只有纯净的、使人欢喜的临在。这位菩萨就是药王菩萨的前身。

In that Buddha's teachings there was a bodhisattva named All-Beings-Delighted-to-See.

The bodhisattva's name is his nature. All-Beings-Delighted-to-See is not called this because he performs spectacular miracles; he is called this because his very presence brings joy. This is what spiritual attainment looks like at the heart level: not pride, not impressive powers, but a fundamental cheerfulness and warmth of being that makes others happy to be near. The sutra is establishing, before telling his story of austerity, that his austerity was in service of this — becoming a being whose presence itself is a blessing.

是菩萨乐习苦行，于日月净明德佛法中，精进经行，一心求佛，满万二千岁已，得现一切色身三昧

这位菩萨乐于修行苦行，在日月净明德佛的教法中精进不退。「精进经行」——精进地行走，精进地修行；「一心求佛」——单一地、毫不分散地追求成佛；「满万二千岁」——修行了12,000年；「得现一切色身三昧」——获得了能够化现各种身形的三昧。修行的长度（12,000年）与最终获得的能力（无限的身体变化）形成对比，暗示：持久的专注修行，会带来无限的灵活性与自在。

This bodhisattva delighted in austere practices. Within the teachings of that Buddha, he advanced diligently without rest, seeking single-mindedly to become a Buddha. After twelve thousand years of practice, he attained the samadhi of being able to manifest all sorts of physical forms.

The progression from austerity to capability is the Lotus Sutra's consistent model: the discipline, the focus, the unwavering commitment of the practice creates the ground for eventual spontaneous flexibility and freedom. "One mind seeking Buddha" (一心求佛) describes not a tense, anxious striving, but a complete orientation of being toward awakening. When such a mind is maintained for twelve thousand years without deviation, it naturally matures into the samadhi of form-changing — the ability to take any shape, to be anything that any being needs. Discipline is the path to freedom; austerity is the door to infinite adaptability.

得此三昧已，心大欢喜，即作念言：我得现一切色身三昧，皆是得闻法华经力

获得了「现一切色身三昧」后，菩萨的心中充满了喜悦。他立刻想到：「我之所以能获得这样的成就，全是因为听闻《法华经》的力量。」这是《法华经》中最核心的主张之一：一切成就，根本上都源于对法的听闻与领纳。不是个人的修行「创造」了成就，而是法的力量通过修行者而显现。

Having attained this samadhi, his mind was filled with great joy. He immediately had this thought: "All my attainment of this ability to manifest all forms comes entirely from the power of hearing the Lotus Sutra."

This moment of recognition is crucial. The bodhisattva does not attribute his attainment to his own effort alone, but explicitly credits the Dharma itself. This is the Lotus Sutra's distinctive understanding: the teaching is alive; it carries power; it is not merely information to be processed but a transformative force that works through the sincere practitioner. The joy (心大欢喜) is the natural response to this recognition — not self-satisfaction, but the joy of knowing that he is aligned with something greater than himself, something that is actually doing the work of liberation.

我今当供养日月净明德佛

获得成就后，菩萨的第一个念头不是自己的修行完成了，而是「我现在应该供养这位佛陀。」这是修行的最高精神：所有成就都是为了服务，所有力量都是为了供养、护持真理与众生。这种反向的感恩——向给予你根本的那个源头供养——使得修行不会陷入自我封闭。

Now I should make an offering to the Buddha Tathagata of Sun and Moon Pure Radiance.

The bodhisattva's first impulse upon attainment is not self-congratulation but gratitude expressed through offering. This is the ethical core of Mahayana practice: attainment is immediately understood as a basis for service, not a resting place. The bodhisattva's powers are not his to keep; they are resources to be deployed in the service of the teaching and the Buddha. This movement — from attainment immediately toward offering and service — ensures that spiritual development does not become narcissistic or self-enclosed. Awakening, in the Lotus Sutra, is always awakening in relation to something beyond oneself.

即以神力，雨众名华，及以香雨，绕佛三匝，以头面礼足，合掌恭敬，烧身供佛

菩萨立刻行动了。「即以神力」——立刻用神通力；「雨众名华」——降下各种名贵的花朵；「及以香雨」——并降下香气之雨；「绕佛三匝」——三次绕佛而行，每一圈都在强化一次尊敬的动作；「以头面礼足」——以头面贴于佛的足下，这是最谦卑的礼节；「合掌恭敬」——合掌表示虔诚；「烧身供佛」——最终，以燃烧自己的身体来供养。整个描述从外在的供物（花、香）逐步深入到身体本身的献祭，最后达到了自我的完全给予。

Immediately, using his miraculous powers, he rained down flowers of many kinds and scented rains upon the Buddha. He circumambulated the Buddha three times, pressed his head and face to the Buddha's feet in deepest reverence, placed his palms together in respect, and set his own body on fire as an offering to the Buddha.

The sequence of offerings moves from the external to the internal, from gifts brought from outside to the gift of oneself. Flowers and fragrant rain are traditional offerings; circumambulation and prostration are expressions of reverence. But the act culminates in self-immolation — the complete, permanent offering of the body. This is not melodrama in the Lotus Sutra's understanding; it is the logical endpoint of non-attachment and devotion. When there is nothing you will not give, when self and other have dissolved into a single impulse of offering, then the body itself becomes fuel for the Dharma. This is why the Buddha praises it: not because suffering is good, but because it represents the complete disidentification with the self that makes liberation possible.

一切众生喜见菩萨——饮香油，燃身供佛

第二十三章讲述了药王菩萨的前身故事。在无量劫前，他是一位名叫「一切众生喜见」的菩萨，修行多年后证得三昧。为了供养佛陀，他喝下各种香油，点火燃烧自己的身体，以燃身作为最高供养，燃烧了一千二百年。这是《法华经》中最震撼的苦行描写。

In a past age, the bodhisattva later known as Medicine King was then called All-Beings-Delighted-to-See. After twelve thousand years of practice and attaining a great samadhi, he drank fragrant oils, wrapped himself in jewelled garments, set himself on fire as an offering to the Buddha, and burned for 1,200 years.

The self-immolation of All-Beings-Delighted-to-See is one of the most challenging passages in the Lotus Sutra — and historically, one of the most influential. It has inspired practices of self-immolation among Buddhist practitioners in China, Korea, and Vietnam across many centuries, most famously Thich Quang Duc in 1963. The text presents the act as the highest possible offering — the complete giving of oneself. In the Lotus Sutra's symbolic universe, the body is not the self; giving it up is not death but the ultimate expression of non-attachment. However interpreted, the passage is unmistakably about the total, unconditional nature of true devotion.

「此真精进，此名真法供养如来」

日月净明德佛在菩萨燃身供佛后，称赞道：「善哉，善哉！这才是真正的精进！这才是真正的法供养！」这句话是理解本章的关键：燃身并不是残忍或自毁，而是在佛陀看来，是「法供养」的极致——将法与供养合一，用自己的生命来印证和传达真理。

The Buddha says: "This is true diligence! This is what is called a true offering of Dharma to the Tathagata!"

The Buddha's endorsement distinguishes the act from mere self-destruction. What makes the burning a "Dharma offering" rather than a suicide is the quality of consciousness behind it: complete non-attachment, total orientation toward the welfare of the Dharma and beings, no residue of self-seeking. The Buddha's words "this is true diligence" (此真精进) suggest that the burning body is an expression of a mind fully aligned with the bodhisattva path — not a desperate act, but the most complete possible offering of the very instrument of practice. Whether one takes this literally or symbolically, the teaching is clear: the highest offering is the offering of oneself, without reservation.

再生后再次燃臂供佛——坚持七万二千岁

燃身之后，菩萨在另一个佛土再度投生，再次回到日月净明德佛前。此时佛陀即将涅槃，嘱咐他守护《法华经》。菩萨为供养佛、供养经，再次燃烧自己的双臂，持续了七万二千岁。这种重复性的舍身，强调了他对法的绝对奉献：一次不够，再来一次。

After being reborn, the bodhisattva returns to the same Buddha's presence and, when the Buddha is about to enter nirvana, burns both arms as an offering for seventy-two thousand years — grieving the Buddha's passing and honouring the Lotus Sutra.

The second burning — of the arms, for seventy-two thousand years — is less about spectacle than about endurance. The first burning was cosmic and transformative; the second is sustained and devotional. It is the long form of the same commitment. The bodhisattva is not performing for an audience; the Buddha is departing, and this is his response to loss: to keep the fire burning, literally and metaphorically, for the duration of the mourning. There is something very human in this — the grief of a devoted practitioner at the teacher's departure, expressed through the only language he knows: complete self-offering.

药王菩萨——以身为药，以法为药

这位菩萨后来转世成为「药王」（Medicine King），以治愈众生的疾苦为己任。他用身体供佛，获得了能够治愈一切众生身心之苦的力量。这是一个深刻的因果关系：彻底的舍身，带来了彻底的给予能力。失去自我执著的人，才能真正为他人带来疗愈。

This bodhisattva later became known as Medicine King (药王), whose vocation is to heal the physical and spiritual suffering of all beings. His capacity to heal arose from his complete self-offering: the loss of all self-concern became the source of infinite healing power.

The connection between self-immolation and healing is not incidental. Medicine King's power to heal is the direct fruit of his practice of non-attachment. When there is no self to protect or advance, the entire being becomes available for the service of others. This is one of the Lotus Sutra's deepest paradoxes: the person who gives everything away most completely becomes most capable of giving. The doctor who has healed their own deepest wounds is most able to treat others. Medicine King is the emblem of this principle: the burned body becomes the healer's body, because the burning was the completion of the practice of non-self.

总结 · Summary

第二十三章讲述了药王菩萨前世的极致供养：饮油燃身一千二百年，再燃双臂七万二千年，以彻底的舍身来供养佛法。佛陀称之为「真精进」、「真法供养」。这个故事揭示了大乘佛教中「舍」的极限：当一个人真正放下对身体和自我的执著，他的给予能力反而变得无限。药王菩萨以燃身供佛，最终成为治愈一切众生的医王。

Chapter 23 presents the most extreme expression of devotion in the Lotus Sutra — the former-life story of Medicine King Bodhisattva, who burned his body for 1,200 years and his arms for 72,000 years as offerings to the Buddha and the Dharma. The Buddha calls it "true diligence" and "true Dharma offering." Whatever one makes of the literal act, the teaching is clear: Medicine King's capacity to heal all beings arose directly from his complete non-attachment to his own body. The greatest giving comes from the most complete letting-go. The healer emerges from the fire.
