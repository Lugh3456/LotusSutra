第二十五章 · 观世音菩萨普门品

妙法莲华经

← 第二十四章 目录 Contents 第二十六章 →

尔时，无尽意菩萨即从座起，偏袒右肩，合掌向佛，而作是言：世尊，观世音菩萨，以何因缘，名观世音？佛告无尽意菩萨：善男子，若有无量百千万亿众生，受诸苦恼，闻是观世音菩萨，一心称名，观世音菩萨即时观其音声，皆得解脱。若有持是观世音菩萨名者，设入大火，火不能烧，由是菩萨威神力故。若为大水所漂，称其名号，即得浅处。若有百千万亿众生，为求金银琉璃车渠马瑙珊瑚琥珀真珠等宝，入于大海，假使黑风吹其船舫，飘堕罗刹鬼国，其中若有乃至一人，称观世音菩萨名者，是诸人等，皆得解脱罗刹之难。

逐句解释

尔时，无尽意菩萨即从座起，偏袒右肩，合掌向佛，而作是言：世尊，观世音菩萨，以何因缘，名观世音？

无尽意菩萨（Inexhaustible Intention Bodhisattva）从座位起身，做出了恭敬的姿态：偏袒右肩（一种敬礼的表示），合掌向佛。他的问题直指本章的中心：为什么这位菩萨叫做「观世音」？「观」——观察；「世音」——世界的声音。名字本身就是他的使命与本质的描述。

At that moment, Inexhaustible Intention Bodhisattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder in respect, placed his palms together, and addressed the Buddha: "Honored One, why is Guanyin Bodhisattva called by this name?"

The form of respect — baring the right shoulder, placing palms together — establishes the solemnity of the question. And the question itself is the pedagogical key: the name is not arbitrary; it describes the bodhisattva's nature and function. The act of asking the question on behalf of the assembly is itself a teaching method; by having someone ask aloud what the reader is wondering, the sutra creates a moment of recognition and deepens engagement.

佛告无尽意菩萨：善男子，若有无量百千万亿众生，受诸苦恼，闻是观世音菩萨，一心称名，观世音菩萨即时观其音声，皆得解脱

佛陀的回答涵盖了观世音菩萨的基本功能：凡是众多被苦难折磨的众生，如果一心称念观世音菩萨的名号，菩萨就会立刻听到他们的声音，并让他们得到解脱。关键词是「即时」——没有延迟，没有选择，没有条件。只要一心称名，就立刻得到回应。这定义了观世音菩萨在佛教宇宙中的角色：无条件的、即刻的、无选择的慈悲。

The Buddha told Inexhaustible Intention: "Good man, if any of the immeasurable, countless, innumerable living beings who are suffering in distress hear of the Bodhisattva Guanyin and single-heartedly call upon her name, then Guanyin will immediately perceive the sounds of their voices and all will be liberated."

The Buddha's answer is absolute in its promise: suffering beings, without qualification; calling her name, with complete sincere intention; immediate perception by the bodhisattva; and complete liberation. The phrase "即时观其音声" — instantly perceiving their voices — places Guanyin in a state of perpetual, perfect attention. She is not far away, not occupied with higher meditations, not dealing with "more important" matters. She is completely present to every cry. This is compassion in the Lotus Sutra's understanding: it is not withheld, not measured, not distributed according to merit. It is available universally and immediately to anyone who asks.

若有持是观世音菩萨名者，设入大火，火不能烧，由是菩萨威神力故

经文列举了各种危难的例子。持念观世音菩萨名号的人，即便陷入熊熊烈火，火焰也烧不了他们。这不是物理上的奇迹，而是一种象征：贪嗔痴的内在之火，也被慈悲的力量所熄灭。「由是菩萨威神力故」——由于菩萨的威力与神通。但真实的「威力」是什么？是她无条件的临在，是她对众生苦难的完全回应。

If anyone holds fast to the name of Guanyin and enters a great fire, the fire will not be able to burn them, due to the awesome power of this bodhisattva.

The seven dangers mentioned in the chapter — fire, flood, sword, demon, chains, robbery, storm — are both literal and symbolic. Fire is the danger of passion, burning desire; water is the danger of being overwhelmed; the sword is violence; demons are malevolent forces; chains are bondage; robbery is being stripped of what one has; storm is chaos and confusion. In each case, Guanyin's name and presence provide protection. The protection is not magical in a supernatural sense; it is the natural consequence of a consciousness so completely oriented toward compassion that it is no longer vulnerable to the forces that destroy unawakened beings. The fire cannot burn you not because fire has lost its power, but because you are no longer identified with the part of you that can burn.

若为大水所漂，称其名号，即得浅处

若有众生被大水冲走，称念观世音名号，就能到达浅处而得以活命。水代表的是被情感、环境、业力冲动所淹没的状态。念诵菩萨名号，就如同找到了一块立足之地——一个足够坚实的、足够清醒的立场，使得被淹没的生命得以浮起、得以呼吸。这是观世音帮助众生的方式：不是从天而降的拯救，而是在混乱中找到清明之处。

If anyone is being swept away by great waters and calls upon her name, they will immediately reach shallow ground and be saved.

Water traditionally represents being overwhelmed — by circumstance, emotion, or karmic force. To be carried away by water is to lose one's footing, one's orientation, one's ability to stand. Calling on Guanyin's name is calling back to one's own clarity, reaching for the ground of awareness beneath the turbulent surface. The "shallow place" (浅处) is not a far-off destination but the very ground one has been standing on all along, just waiting to be recognized. This is the Lotus Sutra's recurring insight: liberation is not something to be acquired from the outside but something to be uncovered within.

若有百千万亿众生，为求金银琉璃车渠马瑙珊瑚琥珀真珠等宝，入于大海，假使黑风吹其船舫，飘堕罗刹鬼国，其中若有乃至一人，称观世音菩萨名者，是诸人等，皆得解脱罗刹之难

大商人为了求取各种宝物而入海，却遇到黑风吹翻船舫，漂流到罗刹鬼国的危险。即使在这样绝望的处境中，只要其中有一个人称念观世音菩萨的名号，整船的人都能得救。这说明两个事实：第一，观世音菩萨对众生的救度是无条件的，不因人数、身份、资格而异；第二，一个人的修行与信心，能够庇护周围的人。这是一个关于相互联系、业力共享的真理。

If countless living beings, seeking treasures — gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, coral, amber, pearls — enter the great sea, and a black wind blows their ships toward a terrible place inhabited by rakshasas, then even if just one person among them calls upon the name of Guanyin Bodhisattva, all those people will be liberated from that danger.

The detail that one person's invocation can save an entire ship's worth of people points to the Lotus Sutra's understanding of interconnection. We are not isolated; our practices affect those around us. One person's wholehearted invocation creates a field of protection that extends to all in proximity. The rakshasas — flesh-eating demons — represent the most extreme threat; the black wind represents chaos that no human skill can overcome. Yet against all these external forces, one sincere call to the compassionate presence is enough. The teaching is both humbling and empowering: you cannot save yourself alone, but your genuine practice ripples outward and can save others.

观世音——「观察世界的声音」

无尽意菩萨问：观世音菩萨为何得此名？佛陀的回答点出了整章的主旨：「观世音」的意思是「观察世界的声音」——他时时聆听众生的苦难呼声，并立刻回应。名字本身就是使命：这位菩萨的整个存在，就是一双永远倾听的耳朵和一颗永远响应的心。

Why is this bodhisattva called "Regarder of the World's Sounds"? Because if countless beings are suffering and in distress, and any one of them single-mindedly calls upon this bodhisattva's name, he will instantly perceive their cries and they will all be liberated.

The name Guanshiyin (观世音) — literally "Observing the World's Sounds" — is itself a complete theological statement. This bodhisattva's defining quality is not power, wisdom, or supernatural ability in the usual sense: it is listening. The Mahayana tradition understands deep compassion as fundamentally a quality of attentiveness — being fully present to the suffering of others, without distance, without defence, without selectivity. Guanyin hears everyone who calls. Not some. Not the worthy. Everyone. This is why she (in East Asian tradition, Guanyin is typically depicted as female) has become the most beloved figure in all of East Asian Buddhism: she is the face of a compassion that is always available, never absent, never too busy.

七难解脱：火、水、风、刀、鬼、枷锁、怨贼

经文列举了七种危难：大火、大水、黑风（海上风暴）、刀剑、罗刹鬼、枷锁、强盗。凡遭遇这七种险境而称念观世音菩萨名号者，皆得解脱。这七难涵盖了古印度人所能想象的最主要的世间危险，象征的含义是：无论什么处境，无论多危急，只要一心称名，帮助就会到来。

Those who call upon Guanyin's name will be freed from seven great dangers: fire, flood, storm at sea, swords and weapons, demons, chains and imprisonment, and bandits.

The seven dangers represent the full spectrum of physical catastrophe known to the ancient world. Fire and flood are elemental; storm at sea was the great peril of the maritime trade routes along which this sutra spread; swords and weapons are the dangers of conflict; demons represent unseen malevolent forces; chains represent imprisonment and loss of freedom; bandits represent the lawless violence of the road. Guanyin's protection is not limited to one kind of suffering or one kind of person. She responds to all of them. The repetitive structure — "if any person in any such danger calls her name, they will be freed" — is itself a kind of liturgy, accumulating a sense of boundless, unconditional availability.

三毒解脱：贪、嗔、痴

除了外在的七种危难，观世音菩萨还能帮助众生脱离内在的三毒：若多贪欲，称念观世音名，贪欲即能消散；若多嗔恚，称名即能离嗔；若多愚痴，称名即能增长智慧。内在与外在的苦难，在观世音的慈悲中，都有应对之道。

Beyond external dangers, Guanyin's compassion also addresses the three inner poisons: calling upon her name can reduce greed (贪), dissipate anger (嗔), and decrease delusion (痴).

The extension of Guanyin's help from external dangers to internal afflictions is a crucial theological move. It shifts her from a rescue-deity (useful in emergencies) to a transformative presence (useful always, in the most fundamental human struggles). The three poisons — greed, hatred, and delusion — are, in Buddhist understanding, the root causes of all suffering. They are also the universal human condition. By saying that calling upon Guanyin can address these, the sutra is saying that she is relevant not just to those in dramatic peril, but to every person, in every moment, struggling with the ordinary suffering of being a human being.

三十三化身——观世音以何身得度，即现何身而为说法

观世音菩萨能化现三十三种身形，为不同类型的众生说法：佛身、辟支佛身、声闻身、梵王身、帝释身、自在天身、将军身、居士身、宰官身、婆罗门身、比丘身、比丘尼身、优婆塞身、优婆夷身、长者妇女身、居士妇女身、宰官妇女身、婆罗门妇女身、童男身、童女身、天龙身、夜叉身等。这是《法华经》对方便法门最完整、最具体的展现。

Guanyin manifests thirty-three different bodily forms to teach beings — from Buddhas and bodhisattvas to monks, nuns, laypeople of every social class and gender, children, and non-human beings. Whatever form a being needs in order to be liberated, she takes that form and speaks to them.

The thirty-three bodies of Guanyin are the most systematic presentation of the doctrine of skillful means in the entire sutra. The list spans the full social hierarchy of ancient India — kings and merchants, brahmins and workers, monks and laypeople, men and women, adults and children. It also spans the cosmological hierarchy — gods, dragons, yakshas. No social category, no rank, no gender is excluded from her reach. The phrase "应以何身得度，即现何身而为说法" — "in whatever form a being can be saved, she manifests that form and teaches them" — is one of the most quoted and most beloved lines in all of Chinese Buddhism.

总结 · Summary

第二十五章「普门品」是《法华经》流传最广的一章，常被单独抄录和诵读，几乎成为独立的经典。「普门」意为「普遍的门」：观世音菩萨为一切众生开启了随时可进的救度之门——不分贫富，不分僧俗，不分性别，不分处境。他（她）聆听一切人的声音，以一切形象回应，解除七难，平息三毒。观世音不是遥远神圣的存在，而是最亲近、最随时可召唤的慈悲。

Chapter 25, the Universal Gate of Guanyin, is the most widely read and recited chapter in the Lotus Sutra — often circulated as an independent text. "Universal Gate" (普门) means that Guanyin's compassion opens to everyone, without condition or qualification. She hears every call, takes whatever form is needed, liberates from every external danger, and addresses every inner affliction. The thirty-three bodies she manifests span the entire range of human and non-human existence. No one is outside her reach. This is why Guanyin has become the most beloved figure in East Asian Buddhism: she is not a remote, abstract divinity but the face of a compassion that is always listening, always near, always ready to respond.
