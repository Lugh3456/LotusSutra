第七章 · 化城喻品

妙法莲华经

← 第六章 目录 Contents 第八章 →

譬如五百由旬，险难恶道，旷绝无人，怖畏之处。若有多众，欲过此道，至珍宝处。有一导师，聪慧明达，善知险道，通塞之相，将导众人，欲过此难。所将人众，中路懈退，白导师言：我等疲极，而复怖畏，不能复进，前路尚远。导师多诸方便，而作是念：此等可愍，云何舍大珍宝而欲退还？作是念已，以方便力，于险道中，过三百由旬，化作一城，告众人言：汝等勿怖，莫得退还。今此大城，可于中止，随意所作。若入是城，快得安隐。若能前至宝所，亦可得去。

逐句解释

譬如五百由旬，险难恶道，旷绝无人，怖畏之处。若有多众，欲过此道，至珍宝处

「化城喻」是《法华经》第四个大比喻。一条漫长危险的道路，长达五百由旬，路上荒无人烟，充满恐怖和危险。一大群人想要穿过这条危险的道路，目的地是远方的宝藏。这条道路象征修行之路的艰辛，那些令人畏惧的因素象征修行中必然面对的困难。

There is a dangerous and difficult road, five hundred leagues long, desolate and uninhabited, a place of fear and dread. A large group of people wish to travel this road to reach a place of great treasure.

The Parable of the Phantom City (化城喻) is the Lotus Sutra's fourth great parable, and it directly addresses a real problem in spiritual practice: exhaustion and the desire to stop before reaching the goal. The five-hundred-league road is the entire path of Buddhist practice across countless lifetimes. The treasure at the end is Buddhahood. The terrain — dangerous, empty, frightening — honestly represents the genuine difficulties of sustained spiritual effort. Unlike the parables of the burning house or the prodigal son, which focus on motivation and recognition, this parable focuses on the middle of the journey, when people run out of energy and want to turn back.

有一导师，聪慧明达，善知险道，通塞之相，将导众人，欲过此难

队伍中有一位导师，他聪明通达，深知这条危险之路的每一处险要和通路。他自愿带领众人穿过这个难关。导师代表佛陀，他的知识和能力来自于曾经走过这条路，甚至不止一次。

There is a wise and understanding guide who knows the dangerous road well, understands where it is passable and where it is not, and wishes to lead the people through.

The guide is not a distant deity but someone who has practical knowledge born of experience. His wisdom (聪慧) and clarity (明达) come from knowing the specific conditions of this road — where it is passable, where the danger is greatest. This grounds the Buddha's teaching in reality: the Buddha is not offering theoretical advice but guidance from someone who has walked this very path. His intention to lead others through — not to stay safe while others suffer — embodies compassion in action.

所将人众，中路懈退，白导师言：我等疲极，而复怖畏，不能复进，前路尚远

走到一半，众人精疲力竭，想要放弃。他们对导师说：我们已经完全疲倦了，而且害怕前路还有更多危险。我们无法再继续走下去了，前面的路还那么远。这不是懒惰，而是真实的疲倦和恐惧。

Halfway through the journey, the people become exhausted and frightened. They tell the guide: We are utterly worn out and afraid. We cannot go on. The road ahead is still so far.

This is one of the most psychologically honest moments in the Lotus Sutra. It does not idealise the spiritual path. Halfway through any genuine transformative journey, there is almost inevitably a crisis of motivation. The initial enthusiasm fades, the goal still seems impossibly distant, and the accumulated effort has not yet yielded the breakthrough that would make it all feel worthwhile. The travellers are not cowards; they have already come a long way. Their exhaustion is real and deserves compassion. The guide's response is not to scold them or push them harder — it is to create a rest stop.

导师多诸方便，而作是念：此等可愍，云何舍大珍宝而欲退还？作是念已，以方便力，于险道中，过三百由旬，化作一城，告众人言：汝等勿怖，莫得退还

导师有很多方便之法。看到众人的疲倦和恐惧，导师陷入深思：这些人真是可怜，他们为什么要舍弃伟大的珍宝而想要退缩呢？导师做出了决定。他用神通和方便之力，在危险的道路中间——已经走了三百由旬之后——变化出了一座城市。然后他告诉众人：不要害怕，不要退缩。这座城是真实的，可以在这里停留休息。

The guide possesses many skillful means. Seeing the people's exhaustion and fear, he reflects: These people are truly pitiful. Why would they abandon the great treasure and wish to turn back? Using his skillful power and miraculous ability, he then conjures a great city in the middle of the dangerous road and tells the travellers: Do not be afraid. Do not turn back. This great city is real, and you may rest here.

The guide's response is not impatience but deep compassion. The phantom city is not deception but medicine. The guide does not pretend the treasure is here; rather, he creates a rest stop that is real enough to restore energy and hope. By creating a place where rest is possible, he preserves the possibility of continuing the journey. Without this rest, the travellers would either turn back or collapse. With it, they can recover and eventually complete their journey. This is the paradox of skillful means: sometimes the truth is not what people can bear; a provisional truth can save them.

今此大城，可于中止，随意所作。若入是城，快得安隐。若能前至宝所，亦可得去

进入这座城后，旅人会很快地得到安全和安隐。但导师也没有隐瞒他们：如果你们能够继续前行到宝藏的地方，那当然更好，也是可以去的。他给了他们两个选择：一是在这座城中充分休息，二是当他们准备好了，继续向宝藏前进。

You may stop and rest in this city at your leisure. If you enter this city, you will quickly find safety and peace. And if you are able to continue onward to the treasure place, that too is possible.

The guide's address to the people balances two truths perfectly. First: rest is legitimate and necessary. The peace offered by the city is not a lie or a trap. Second: the treasure is real and still ahead. The city is not the final destination. This holds both realities in tension without collapsing them into one. A great teacher knows when to say "stay" and when to say "go." The guide's power lies in knowing that the moment will come when his words shift from one to the other.

总结 · Summary

「化城喻」是一个关于中途倦怠与方便引导的比喻。导师变化出一座城，让筋疲力竭的旅人休息，然后在他们恢复之后，才告诉他们：这座城是我造出来的，真正的宝藏还在前方。在《法华经》的语境中，「化城」象征声闻乘所证的涅槃——它是真实的，是需要的，但不是终点。真正的终点是佛果，而佛陀作为导师，会带着每一个疲倦的旅人最终抵达那里。

The Parable of the Phantom City addresses one of the most human aspects of the spiritual path: the mid-journey crisis. When exhaustion sets in and the goal seems impossibly distant, the guide creates a rest stop — real enough for recovery, provisional enough to be left behind. In the Lotus Sutra's framework, this phantom city represents the nirvana of early Buddhism: a genuine peace, a real liberation from suffering, but not the ultimate destination. The ultimate treasure is Buddhahood. The guide's patience and skillful means ensure that no one who sets out on the path is permanently left behind. Rest when you need to. The road continues. The treasure is real.
