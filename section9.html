第九章 · 授学无学人记品

妙法莲华经

← 第八章 目录 Contents 第十章 →

尔时，阿难、罗睺罗而作是念：我等每自思惟，设得授记，不亦快乎！即从座起，到于佛前，头面礼足，俱白佛言：世尊，我等于此，亦应有分。唯有如来，我等所归，又我等为一切世间天人阿修罗所见知识。阿难常为侍者，护持法藏，罗睺罗是佛之子。若佛见授阿耨多罗三藐三菩提记者，我愿既满，众望亦足。

逐句解释

尔时，阿难、罗睺罗而作是念：我等每自思惟，设得授记，不亦快乎！

阿难和罗睺罗为什么要站起来请求授记？经文告诉我们，他们看着其他弟子一个个得到授记，内心生起了渴望。这种渴望不是贪欲，而是对解脱之道真诚的向往。两位弟子在心中反复思考：如果我们也能得到授记，那会多么值得高兴啊！

Ananda and Rahula, watching the prophecies being given to others, think to themselves: What joy it would be if we too received a prophecy!

This inner moment of longing is crucial. The text allows us inside their minds, hearing their thought rather than their external action first. In the Lotus Sutra's pedagogy, sincere aspiration — even before any external expression — is the beginning of awakening. The fact that they "think" this before they "do" it emphasises that the vow comes from authentic desire, not from social convention or fear.

即从座起，到于佛前，头面礼足，俱白佛言：世尊，我等于此，亦应有分

他们的内心渴望化作了行动。两位高德的弟子，在众人面前，站起身来走到佛陀的座前，以最恭敬的礼仪向佛陀顶礼，然后一起说出了那句简洁而有力的话：「世尊，我等于此，亦应有分。」——世尊，我们在这里面也应该有一份。这个请求是坦诚的，毫无遮掩。

They rise from their seats, approach the Buddha, and bow at his feet. Together they say: World-Honoured One, surely we too should have a share in this.

The action is public and unambiguous. These are not marginal figures hesitantly requesting; these are senior members of the sangha, known for their virtue and learning, openly expressing their longing. The phrase "we too" (我等) emphasises the natural justice of their request — if others receive prophecies, why not them? The question is not asked in a spirit of complaint but of simple equality. In the context of the Lotus Sutra's one vehicle, this equality is the point.

唯有如来，我等所归，又我等为一切世间天人阿修罗所见知识

阿难和罗睺罗接着说出了他们请求的理由。他们说：我们只有归向如来；而且，我们在世间被众人所知晓。这里有两层意思：首先，他们的根本皈依是佛陀，没有别的依靠；其次，他们在世间有一定的地位和影响力，他们的身份本身就是传法的工具。他们既表达了对佛陀的绝对信心，也暗示他们有能力利用自己的位置为法服务。

We have no refuge but the Tathagata. And we are known and recognised by all beings in the world — by gods, humans, and asuras.

Ananda and Rahula ground their request in two foundations. First: their absolute commitment to the Buddha (唯有如來) — they are not asking for prophecy as a reward among many available goods, but as a recognition of their deepest orientation. Second: their standing in the world — they are not hidden figures, but visible, respected teachers. This is not arrogance; it is acknowledgment of responsibility. They are asking: we are in a position to serve the dharma; please confirm that we will ultimately reach the goal.

阿难常为侍者，护持法藏，罗睺罗是佛之子

这里列举了阿难和罗睺罗各自的独特身份。阿难「常为侍者，护持法藏」——他一直是佛陀的贴身侍者，同时也是法的护持者和承载者。罗睺罗「是佛之子」——这个身份在世俗上给了他特殊的地位。但在《法华经》的语境中，这些身份的提及不是为了显示特权，而是为了说明：他们各自在特定的角色中，对法的传承与实践都有深刻的责任。

Ananda constantly serves as the Buddha's attendant, protecting and preserving the dharma treasury. Rahula is the Buddha's own son.

These two descriptors point to two different relationships with the teaching. Ananda's role as attendant and keeper of the dharma makes him the repository of the Buddha's words; his memory is legendary in Buddhist tradition — at the first council after the Buddha's death, he will recite all the sutras from memory. Rahula's identity as the Buddha's son speaks to a different dimension: the intimacy of family, the teaching that happens in proximity to the awakened one. Neither role is superior to the other; they are two complementary ways of being close to the truth.

若佛见授阿耨多罗三藐三菩提记者，我愿既满，众望亦足

阿难和罗睺罗的请求到此达到了高潮。他们说：如果佛陀给我们授记成就无上圆满正等正觉，那么我们的愿望就彻底满足了，众人的期望也就圆满了。「我愿既满，众望亦足」这八个字，优美而有力地总结了他们的整个请求。他们不只是为自己而请，也是为了众人的信心而请。

If the Buddha would grant us prophecy of supreme perfect enlightenment, then our deepest wish will be fulfilled, and the hopes of all will be satisfied.

The phrase "my wish will be fulfilled and the hopes of all will be satisfied" (我願既滿，眾望亦足) elegantly closes the request. It connects the personal (their own longing for prophecy) with the universal (the collective hope of the assembly). They are essentially saying: when you confirm our buddhahood, you confirm the truth that all beings deserve the same hope. It is a completely selfless ending to what might otherwise be a self-interested petition.

「有学」与「无学」——两种修行阶段

本章题目中的「学」指尚在修学阶段的人（有学，sekha），「无学」指已经完成修学、证得阿罗汉果的人（asekha）。佛陀在这一章同时向这两类人授记成佛，展示了《法华经》「一乘」理念的彻底性：无论你在修行路上走了多远，授记都在等着你。

"Those with further training" (有学, sekha) refers to practitioners who have entered the path but not yet completed it. "Those beyond training" (無學, asekha) refers to arhats who have completed the path and attained liberation. This chapter grants prophecies of Buddhahood to both.

The Pali Buddhist tradition uses "sekha" and "asekha" as technical terms. A sekha has entered the path (at minimum, the first stage of stream-entry) but still has work to do. An asekha has completed all the training and is free from the cycle of rebirth. The Lotus Sutra's decision to address both groups together is inclusive by design: the invitation to Buddhahood is not reserved for those who have "graduated." It is extended to everyone on the path, at every stage. The chapter essentially says: wherever you are on the journey, the same destination awaits.

阿难未来成佛，号山海慧自在通王如来

佛陀接受了他们的请求，给阿难授记：未来成佛，号「山海慧自在通王如来」。这个名号意义深远——「山」的坚稳、「海」的广博、「慧」的通达、「自在」的无碍。正是阿难一生的写照：以无比的记忆力守护了整个佛法，如山如海般宽广厚重。

The Buddha grants Ananda a prophecy: he will become a Buddha named Mountain Sea Wisdom Unimpeded Power King Tathagata.

Ananda's Buddha-name is a compressed portrait of his qualities. "Mountain" suggests the immovable stability of his memory and devotion. "Sea" evokes the vast depth of his learning. "Wisdom" points to the clarity he will develop across countless future lifetimes. "Unimpeded Power" speaks to the freedom of full Buddhahood. Ananda spent his entire life as the Buddha's attendant, memorising every discourse, every conversation, every detail. At the First Buddhist Council after the Buddha's death, he recited the entirety of the sutras from memory — thus giving us the Dharma we have today. His prophecy feels earned not as a reward but as a natural unfolding of who he already is.

罗睺罗亦得授记，未来成佛号蹈七宝华如来

罗睺罗是释迦牟尼出家前的亲生儿子，后来也成为了弟子，以「密行第一」著称——他的修行深沉低调，不显山露水。他得到的佛号是「蹈七宝华如来」，意象优美，与他内敛而坚实的修行风格相符。父子二人，一起成佛，在轮回中再度相遇。

Rahula also receives a prophecy. He will become a Buddha named Treading on Seven-Jewel Flowers Tathagata.

Rahula (罗睺罗) was Siddhartha Gautama's son, born the very night his father left home to seek enlightenment. In Buddhist tradition, Rahula is honoured as "foremost in secret practice" (密行第一) — his spiritual practice was deep, private, and not outwardly showy. His Buddha-name, "Treading on Seven-Jewel Flowers," is an image of quiet grace: each step falling on precious blossoms. The scene of father and son — Shakyamuni Buddha and Rahula — both present in this assembly, with Rahula receiving his prophecy of future Buddhahood, has a particular emotional resonance. The path that once took a father away from his family now reunites them in the deepest possible way: as fellow wayfarers toward the same destination.

总结 · Summary

第九章授记给阿难和罗睺罗——一位是贴身侍者，一位是亲生儿子。这两个人各自代表着不同的修行品质（阿难的博闻强记、罗睺罗的密行低调），都得到了佛陀郑重的成佛预言。本章传达的信息很简单：无论你是公开的还是低调的修行者，无论你是「老修行」还是「新学人」，成佛的门对你敞开。渴望是真实的，回应也是真实的。

Chapter 9 grants prophecies to Ananda and Rahula — the Buddha's personal attendant and his own son. Their moment of stepping forward and simply asking "may we have a share in this?" is one of the most human passages in the sutra. The Buddha's response is immediate and generous. Ananda, the great memoriser and keeper of the Dharma, will become Mountain Sea Wisdom Unimpeded Power King. Rahula, the quiet and private practitioner, will become Treading on Seven-Jewel Flowers. Two very different personalities, two different styles of practice, one destination. The chapter confirms that the sutra's promise is not abstract — it is personal, specific, and addressed to each individual who sincerely aspires.
